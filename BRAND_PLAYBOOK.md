## 1. Brand foundation

### 1.1 One-liner
**The modern platform to run your martial arts academy.**

Tagline shorter (social, app stores): **Run your academy. Not your spreadsheets.**

### 1.2 Mission
Give martial arts academy owners the time, control, and financial clarity
they've never had — by replacing spreadsheets, group chats, and clunky
generic gym software with a tool built by people who train.

### 1.3 Vision
Become the operating system for the next generation of US martial arts
academies. By 2028, if you walk into any BJJ, MMA, or Muay Thai gym in the
country, the front desk runs Fit4Academy.

### 1.4 Positioning statement
- **For**: owners and operators of martial arts academies in the United States.
- **Who**: today juggle attendance sheets, group texts, paper waivers, and
  generic gym software that wasn't built for ranks, stripes, gi/no-gi, or
  belt promotions.
- **Fit4Academy is**: a CRM and operations platform built natively for
  martial arts.
- **That**: handles members, ranks, classes, attendance, billing, events,
  retail, and AI-driven business insights — in English, Portuguese, and
  Spanish, on one screen.
- **Unlike Mindbody, Glofox, or ZenPlanner**: it speaks the language of the
  mat (belts, stripes, gi/no-gi, open mat, smoker tournaments, kid-class
  vs adult-class workflows), at a fair price, with a setup time measured
  in minutes — not weeks.

### 1.5 Brand values
1. **Mat first** — the people building the product train. Every decision is
   measured against "would this make sense to a coach with a black belt and
   a small business?"
2. **Simple by default, powerful when needed** — the easy path has to work,
   without reading docs.
3. **No tech excuse** — if the owner can't use Excel formulas, they can
   still run their entire academy on Fit4Academy.
4. **Multi-lingual is non-negotiable** — English, Portuguese, Spanish are
   first-class. Translation is not a feature; it's the floor.
5. **Honest pricing** — flat platform fee ($0.30/transaction), no hidden
   markup, no per-member surprise bills. We say it out loud.
6. **Respect the legacy** — Jiu-Jitsu and martial arts are older than us.
   Our brand never trivializes the lineage, the belts, or the people who
   came before.

### 1.6 Brand archetype
**The Coach** (a hybrid of Hero and Sage).
- Like a coach, we push the customer to grow.
- Like a coach, we know when to be tough and when to be patient.
- Unlike a guru, we don't sell vague enlightenment — we hand over a
  scoreboard and a play.

This shows up in copy as: confident, second-person ("you"), action-first
verbs, no fluff. Never patronizing. Never bro-culture.

### 1.7 Brand promise
> *"You'll close the laptop on Friday knowing exactly who paid, who's
> ranking up, and who needs a call. Without a spreadsheet."*

Every feature should make that promise more true.

## 2. Origin story & narrative

### 2.1 The story (for site, deck, sales calls)
Fit4Academy was built because the people running martial arts gyms — most
of them coaches first, business owners second — were drowning in admin.
Existing software was either too expensive (Mindbody at $169+/month with
add-ons), too generic (built for yoga and Pilates, not for tracking grau
on a faixa-roxa), or too thin (a check-in app with no billing).

We wanted one tool. Simple enough that the front desk could learn it in
an hour. Smart enough that the owner could see, on a Sunday night, exactly
how the gym performed last week. Multilingual because half of US BJJ is
Brazilian. Honest about pricing because the community talks.

We built it. We named it Fit4Academy.

### 2.2 Narrative pillars (use in marketing)
Every public message ladders up to one of these four pillars:

| Pillar | What we say | Proof point |
|---|---|---|
| **Built for the mat** | Belts, stripes, gi/no-gi, kids vs adults, all native | Default programs, belt system, promotion workflow |
| **One screen, no spreadsheets** | Members, billing, classes, store, events — together | 135+ routes in one app |
| **Multilingual by design** | English, Portuguese, Spanish from day one | i18n.py is 90KB |
| **Honest economics** | Flat $0.30/transaction, no per-member fees | Public pricing |

### 2.3 The "why now"
- The US has the largest non-Brazilian BJJ market in the world (~3M+ practitioners).
- ~30% of new gyms in 2020-2025 were martial arts. Mindbody and Glofox lost share because they were never built for ranks.
- AI lowered the cost of giving every gym owner a "business advisor" — Marcos AI is the personification of that shift.

## 3. Audience

### 3.1 Primary persona — "Coach Marco"
- Male, 35-50, BJJ black belt or brown belt.
- Owns or co-owns a gym with 60-300 active members.
- Tried Mindbody once, canceled in 60 days. Tried Glofox, never finished setup.
- Currently runs the gym on: a shared Google Sheet, WhatsApp groups, paper sign-in clipboard, Stripe Dashboard for cards, Square for in-person.
- Often Brazilian or first-generation American; thinks in Portuguese, sells in English.
- Lives in: Texas, Florida, California, Arizona, New York, North Carolina, Georgia (top US BJJ states).
- **Top pain**: "I know who owes me, but it takes me 3 hours every Tuesday to chase them down."
- **Top dream**: "Coach more. Email less."

### 3.2 Secondary persona — "Manager Patricia"
- Female, 28-40, hired by the owner to run the front desk.
- Already digitally fluent (Instagram, Canva, Notion).
- Speaks the gym's primary language plus English; bilingual is a job requirement.
- **Top pain**: "Every report I need has to come from the owner's spreadsheet, which is a disaster, and I can't touch it."
- **Top dream**: a clean dashboard she controls, automated payment reminders, ready-to-print waivers.

### 3.3 Tertiary persona — "Coach Mike (kids program lead)"
- Male or female, 25-40, brown/black belt or specialized coach.
- Runs the kids programs (the cash cow at most US BJJ gyms).
- **Top pain**: parent communication. Permissions, attendance, belt graduations.
- **Top dream**: parents see their kid's progress without him having to text them.

### 3.4 Buyer vs user
- **Buyer**: the owner (Coach Marco). Gets sold on time saved + revenue clarity.
- **Daily user**: the manager + assistant coaches.
- **Indirect users**: members and parents (via the member portal, payment links, event landing pages).
- **Key implication**: marketing speaks to the owner; product UX has to delight the manager; member-facing screens (portal, payment) have to look professional enough that the owner is proud to share them.

### 3.5 Geographic priorities (year 1-2)
1. **Texas, Florida** (largest BJJ markets, Brazilian-heavy diaspora) — primary.
2. **California, Arizona, Nevada** (Spanish + BJJ overlap) — primary.
3. **New York, New Jersey, Massachusetts** (East Coast BJJ scene) — secondary.
4. **North Carolina, Georgia** (rising BJJ states) — secondary.
5. Outside the US — only via inbound, no paid acquisition.

## 4. Competitive positioning

### 4.1 Competitive landscape

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness vs us |
|---|---|---|
| **Mindbody** | Huge brand, full payments | Built for yoga/spa, expensive ($169+), bloated, slow |
| **Glofox / ABC Fitness** | Modern UI | Generic gym, no rank tracking, complex pricing |
| **ZenPlanner** | Long-time martial arts focus | Old UI, weak mobile, weak BJJ-specific (more karate-era) |
| **Kicksite** | Cheap, traditional martial arts | Old-school feel, weak design, weak BJJ |
| **Spark Membership** | Newer, growing | Less feature depth, no AI, no native multilingual |
| **Martialytics** | Martial arts native | Limited footprint, no native billing |
| **Spreadsheets + WhatsApp** | Free, familiar | The actual #1 competitor; we replace this |

### 4.2 Our wedge
1. **BJJ-native belt/stripe system** — competitors treat ranks as a custom field. We treat them as first-class objects.
2. **Multilingual core** — not Google Translate; real PT and ES UI and content.
3. **Marcos AI** — first martial-arts-aware business co-pilot. Tells you what to do with the data, not just shows it.
4. **Clear pricing** — flat $0.30/transaction. No "starter / pro / scale" maze.
5. **Modern visual identity** — looks like Linear / Stripe, not like 2014 SaaS.

### 4.3 What we are not
- ❌ A general fitness app for end-users (we're B2B).
- ❌ A streaming platform for technique videos (we're operations).
- ❌ A booking widget for one-on-one classes (we're full-stack academy management).
- ❌ A free product (we charge $0.30 per transaction; trial is 30 days, then platform fees apply).

### 4.4 The price story
- Trial: **30 days, no credit card required**.
- After trial: **$0.30 per transaction processed through Fit4Academy**.
- That's it. No per-member fee, no platform monthly subscription.
- The line we use: *"You only pay when you get paid."*

(Note: the underlying Stripe processing fees are passed through transparently
in the receipt to the academy owner.)

## 5. Naming system

### 5.1 The name
**Fit4Academy**
- Always written in TitleCase, no space, no hyphen.
- The "4" is intentional — modern US tech naming convention (Web3, AI4Good).
- Stylized variants in copy/UI: "Fit**4**Academy" with the 4 in brand green is allowed. No other stylization.
- Pronounced: "Fit four Academy."

### 5.2 Domains & handles
| Channel | Handle |
|---|---|
| Primary domain | fit4academy.com |
| Email | hello@fit4academy.com (general), support@, sales@, billing@ |
| Instagram | @fit4academy |
| TikTok | @fit4academy |
| YouTube | @fit4academy |
| Twitter/X | @fit4academy |
| LinkedIn | /company/fit4academy |
| GitHub (eng brand) | github.com/fit4academy |

(Verify availability and reserve before launching public.)

### 5.3 Sub-brands
| Sub-brand | What | Naming rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Marcos AI** | The in-app AI business advisor | Always "Marcos AI" first mention, "Marcos" thereafter. Never lowercase. |
| **Fit4Academy Portal** | Member-facing media portal | "Member Portal" in product UI, "Fit4Academy Portal" in marketing |
| **Fit4Academy Pay** | Future: standalone payment processing | Reserved name, do not use yet |
| **Fit4Academy Studio** | Future: marketing/social tools | Reserved name |

### 5.4 Feature naming rules
- **English first** in product UI, then translate. Translations are not literal — they're localized.
- Use the verb the customer uses, not the verb the engineer uses:
  - ✅ "Promote member" (verb the coach uses)
  - ❌ "Update belt rank field"
- Avoid product-marketing jargon ("Engagement Engine", "Retention Booster"). Just say what it does.

### 5.5 Acronyms & internal language
| Term | Use | Don't use |
|---|---|---|
| Member | The student / customer of the academy | "User" (that's the staff side) |
| Staff | Coach + manager + assistant | "Admin" (too tech) |
| Academy | The gym | "Studio", "Box", "Dojo" (only if it's a karate gym specifically) |
| Belt | Rank | "Level", "Tier" |
| Stripe (the company) | Always "Stripe (the payment processor)" first mention, to disambiguate from a BJJ stripe |
| Stripe (BJJ) | "Grau" (PT) / "stripe" (EN) — context will clarify |

## 6. Voice & tone

### 6.1 Voice — the constants
- **Confident** — we know what we're doing. We don't hedge unnecessarily.
- **Direct** — short sentences. Active voice.
- **Human** — conversational. Contractions ("you're", "it's", "won't").
- **Knowledgeable about the sport** — we say "open mat" not "drop-in session", "smoker" not "internal competition".
- **Inclusive** — across martial arts (BJJ, MMA, Muay Thai, etc.), across genders, across belts.
- **Confident, not arrogant** — never "we revolutionized…", never "the industry's #1".

### 6.2 Tone — varies by context

| Context | Tone | Example (English) |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Warm, motivating | "Welcome. Let's get your gym up and running in 5 minutes." |
| User error | Calm, helpful | "That email's already in use. Want to log in instead?" |
| Billing UI | Sober, factual | "3 members are 7+ days late on payment." |
| Marketing copy | Energetic, with edge | "Stop chasing Venmo screenshots. Get paid on time, every time." |
| Customer support | Empathetic, patient | "Tell us what happened. We'll figure it out." |
| Status / system | Plain, technical | "Connection lost. Retrying…" |
| Celebration | Brief, sincere | "First member added. You're live." |

### 6.3 What we never sound like
- ❌ Corporate/legalese: "We at Fit4Academy strive to deliver…"
- ❌ Tech-bro hype: "Disrupting the martial arts vertical…"
- ❌ Generic gym ad copy: "Your fitness journey starts here!"
- ❌ Bro-culture: "Only the strong survive on Fit4Academy."
- ❌ Sage-y mystical: "Honor your journey…"

### 6.4 Multilingual voice
The voice rules apply across languages. Localized voice notes:

**Português (Brasil)**:
- Use "você" sempre, nunca "tu" ou "o senhor".
- Frases curtas, mesma energia direta.
- Usar termos consagrados de Jiu-Jitsu sem traduzir: gi, no-gi, open mat, faixa-azul, grau.
- Evitar gírias regionais que não viajam (não usar "bah", "uai", "mano" excessivo).

**Español (LATAM neutro)**:
- "Tú" en marketing, "tú" o "usted" en transactional formal (preferir "tú").
- Spanish-American spellings, not Castilian (use "computadora", not "ordenador").
- Términos consagrados de Jiu-Jitsu sin traducir: gi, no-gi, open mat, cinturón azul, grau o stripe.

### 6.5 The 6 second test
If a member or owner only reads the first 6 seconds of any piece (subject
line, hero, push notification): they should know **what it is** and **what
to do next**. If they don't, rewrite it.

## 7. Logo system

### 7.1 The marks
| Mark | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Primary mark** | Shield icon containing "F4" in brand green. The default. |
| **Wordmark** | "Fit4Academy" written in Space Grotesk Bold, with "Academy" in green. Use when there's horizontal real estate. |
| **Lockup** | Shield + wordmark side by side. App headers, business cards, deck title slides. |
| **Monogram** | Just "F4" in green gradient. Use only for tiny contexts (favicon, app icon at <32px). |
| **Mono (black/white)** | Single-color version for printing on swag, sponsorship banners, low-color reproductions. |

### 7.2 Construction
- Shield ratio: 1:1 (always square viewbox).
- F4 letterforms: Space Grotesk **Black/800**, letter-spacing -3.
- Inner shield offset: 1.5px stroke at 0.4 opacity for depth.
- Accent bar under the F4: 70w × 3h, 60% opacity.
- See `static/logo-fit4academy.svg` for the source.

### 7.3 Clear space
**Minimum clear space = 0.5 × the height of the mark**, on all sides. No
text, image, border, or other logo allowed inside that zone.

### 7.4 Minimum sizes
| Mark | Digital min | Print min |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 24px | 8mm |
| Wordmark | 120px wide | 30mm wide |
| Lockup | 160px wide | 40mm wide |

### 7.5 Don'ts
- ❌ Don't stretch, skew, or rotate.
- ❌ Don't change the gradient direction (always 135° top-left to bottom-right).
- ❌ Don't recolor outside the brand palette.
- ❌ Don't add drop shadow, glow, bevel, or 3D extrusion.
- ❌ Don't put on busy photos without a darkening overlay.
- ❌ Don't combine with other logos without proper clear space.
- ❌ Don't outline the logo.

### 7.6 Co-branding (with academies)
When showing a partner academy's logo next to ours:
- Both logos same height (use shield height as anchor).
- Vertical divider line between them at 50% opacity.
- Clear space rule applies to both.
- Order: Fit4Academy first if we're hosting; partner first if we're guesting.

## 8. Color

### 8.1 Primary palette

| Name | Hex | Use | Don't use for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mint Green** | `#00DC82` | Primary CTAs, brand accents, links, success | Body text |
| **Mint Dark** | `#00B368` | Gradient endpoint, hover states | Solo without primary |
| **Mint Deep** | `#059669` | Press materials, single-color print | Digital backgrounds |
| **Mint Light** | `#6ee7b7` | Highlight text on dark backgrounds | CTA buttons (low contrast) |
| **Cyan Accent** | `#22d3ee` | Hero gradient text pair, info states | Primary actions |

### 8.2 Neutrals (UI scale)

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Slate 950 | `#0a0f1a` | Public deep dark (payment page, hero overlays) |
| Slate 900 | `#0f172a` | Default dark mode background |
| Slate 800 | `#1e293b` | Cards in dark mode |
| Slate 700 | `#475569` | Body text on light bg |
| Slate 500 | `#64748b` | Secondary text, captions |
| Slate 400 | `#94a3b8` | Disabled text, placeholders |
| Slate 200 | `#e2e8f0` | Dividers, input borders |
| Slate 50 | `#f8fafc` | Input backgrounds, subtle hovers |
| White | `#ffffff` | Card backgrounds, base canvas |

### 8.3 Semantic palette

| State | Hex | Used in |
|---|---|---|
| Success | `#00DC82` (primary) | Payment OK, member active |
| Warning | `#f59e0b` | Past due, low stock |
| Danger | `#ef4444` | Failed charge, hard error |
| Info | `#22d3ee` | Tooltips, neutral notifications |

### 8.4 Signature gradient
```css
linear-gradient(135deg, #00DC82 0%, #00B368 100%)
```
Use on:
- Logo F4 fill
- Primary CTA buttons
- Decorative hero accents
- Brand background panels (subtle, with overlay)

For text gradients (e.g. the word "Academy" in the hero):
```css
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #6ee7b7 0%, #22d3ee 100%);
-webkit-background-clip: text;
-webkit-text-fill-color: transparent;
```

### 8.5 Color rules
- **The 60-30-10 rule**: 60% neutral, 30% secondary neutral, 10% brand
  primary. Do not flood any screen with green.
- **Green is for action and confirmation.** Never for errors, warnings, or
  cautionary messaging.
- **Backgrounds carry mood.** Light mode = clarity (financial, member
  data). Dark mode = focus (live check-in, public payment).
- **Always test contrast** — text on green must hit WCAG AA (4.5:1 for
  body, 3:1 for large). White text on `#00DC82` is borderline; prefer
  `#0f172a` text on `#00DC82` for buttons.

## 9. Typography

### 9.1 Type system

| Family | Role | Weights | Where it shows up |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Space Grotesk** | Display + UI emphasis | 500, 600, 700, 800 | Headings, numbers, buttons, navbar brand |
| **DM Sans** | Body + UI workhorse | 400, 500, 600, 700 | Paragraphs, labels, forms, lists |
| **Inter** | Fallback (public payment, embeds) | 400, 500, 600, 700 | Lighter touch needed |
| **System monospace** | Code-like, IDs | regular | Transaction IDs, debug, copy-token |

Standard import:
```html
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=DM+Sans:opsz,wght@9..40,400;9..40,500;9..40,600;9..40,700&family=Space+Grotesk:wght@500;600;700;800&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
```

### 9.2 Type scale

| Token | Size | Line-height | Weight | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display XL | 48-56px | 1.05 | 800 | Hero only |
| Display | 40-44px | 1.10 | 700 | Marketing section heads |
| H1 | 28-32px | 1.15 | 700 | Page title |
| H2 | 22-24px | 1.20 | 700 | Section title |
| H3 | 18-20px | 1.30 | 600 | Subsection |
| H4 | 16px | 1.40 | 600 | Card title |
| Body L | 16-17px | 1.55 | 400 | Long form |
| Body | 14-15px | 1.55 | 400-500 | Default UI body |
| Body S | 13px | 1.50 | 400 | Auxiliary |
| Caption | 11-12px | 1.40 | 600 | Tag, badge, label |
| Eyebrow | 11px | 1.20 | 700 ALL CAPS letterspacing 0.5 | Section over-title |

### 9.3 Letter-spacing
- Display & H1-H2: `-0.5px` to `-1px` (tight, modern)
- H3-H4: `0`
- Body: `0`
- Eyebrow / all-caps caption: `+0.3px` to `+0.5px`

### 9.4 Numerals
Numbers (revenue, member counts, dashboards) **always** in Space Grotesk
700 or 800. They are a core brand element. They should feel proud.

### 9.5 Don'ts
- ❌ Space Grotesk italic (not designed for it).
- ❌ More than two families per screen.
- ❌ All-caps body paragraphs.
- ❌ Justified text (rivers in narrow columns).
- ❌ Comic Sans, period.

## 10. Iconography & imagery

### 10.1 Icon system
- **Bootstrap Icons** (`bi-*`) is the official set. Already loaded in all templates.
- Default size: **18px**. Inside CTAs: **16-18px**. Inline with body: **14-16px** matched to font-size.
- Default color inherits from text. Active or branded state: `#00DC82`.
- Icons paired with text whenever possible (a11y + clarity).
- Custom icons (when Bootstrap doesn't cover) follow the same stroke weight (~1.5-2px) and outline style.

### 10.2 Photography — direction
**Authentic mat photography**, not stock fitness.

DO:
- Real training rooms with worn mats.
- Sweat, gi marks, real ranks visible.
- Diverse practitioners (gender, age, ethnicity, body type, ability — including kids and adaptive athletes).
- Coaches in their element: teaching, demonstrating, in conversation.
- Candid moments: post-roll handshake, line-up bow, parent watching kid promote.

DON'T:
- ❌ Generic gym/CrossFit/yoga stock.
- ❌ Posed muscle-flex shots.
- ❌ AI-generated images with bad anatomy.
- ❌ Anyone wearing a belt they didn't earn.
- ❌ Mat shots that are clearly fake (props, no logos, no coach in frame).
- ❌ Overly aggressive "fight" framing — we're sport, not violence.

### 10.3 Photography — treatment
Apply this overlay to any photo in marketing context:
```css
linear-gradient(180deg, rgba(15,23,42,0.85) 0%, rgba(30,27,75,0.75) 50%, rgba(15,23,42,0.9) 100%)
```
This darkens unevenly and unifies any photo into our brand.

For the public payment page (`payment_link.html`) and dashboards: no
photography. Plain backgrounds only.

### 10.4 Illustration style
When a photo isn't right (empty states, error pages, abstract concepts):
- Geometric, simple, single-color or duo-color (mint + slate).
- Stroke-based, not filled blobs.
- Inspired by Untitled UI, Linear's icon system. Never mascot-style.
- Animated only when it serves the message (avoid Lottie noise).

### 10.5 Source priorities
1. **Original photography** of partner academies (preferred — get releases signed).
2. **Unsplash** with specific queries: `bjj`, `jiu-jitsu`, `martial arts mat`, `gi training`, `kids martial arts`. Vet for authenticity.
3. **Pexels** as backup.
4. **Generative AI** only for abstract backgrounds, never people.

Hover: `transform: translateY(-1px); box-shadow: 0 8px 24px rgba(0,220,130,0.35);`
Disabled: `opacity: 0.5; cursor: not-allowed; transform: none;`

### 11.2 Button — secondary
- Light bg: `1.5px solid #e2e8f0`, `bg #fff`, `color #00DC82`.
- Dark bg: `1.5px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1)`, `bg transparent`, `color #6ee7b7`.

### 11.3 Button — ghost / tertiary
Only icon + text. No border. `color: #475569` (light) / `#94a3b8` (dark).
Hover: `bg: rgba(0,220,130,0.06)`.

### 11.4 Input field
```css
border: 1.5px solid #e2e8f0;
background: #f8fafc;
border-radius: 12px;
padding: 14px;
font-size: 15px;
font-family: 'DM Sans', sans-serif;
```
Focus: `border-color: #00DC82; box-shadow: 0 0 0 3px rgba(0,220,130,0.08); background: #fff;`
Error: `border-color: #ef4444;` + helper text below in `#ef4444`.

### 11.5 Card
- Background: `#fff` (light) or `rgba(15,25,40,0.8)` (dark, semi-transparent).
- Border: `1px solid #e2e8f0` / `rgba(255,255,255,0.06)`.
- Border-radius: **18-20px** (large), **12px** (compact list items).
- Padding: 24-32px (large), 16-20px (compact).
- Shadow: `0 4px 16px rgba(0,0,0,0.05)` (light), `0 20px 60px rgba(0,0,0,0.5)` (dark).

### 11.6 Badge / pill
```css
padding: 5px 12px;
border-radius: 999px (pill) or 8px (squared);
font-size: 11-12px;
font-weight: 700;
letter-spacing: 0.3px;
```
Variants: success (green), warning (amber), danger (red), info (cyan), neutral (slate).

### 11.7 Modal
- Backdrop: `rgba(15,23,42,0.7)` with `backdrop-filter: blur(8px)`.
- Card: 18-20px radius, max-width 480px (default), centered.
- Close: top-right, plain `bi-x` icon.
- Primary action right, secondary/cancel left or above.

### 11.8 Liquid glass (optional decoration)
Defined in `static/liquid-glass.css`. Use **only** for hero accents,
celebration moments, or marketing surfaces. Never for forms, tables, or
high-density data screens (legibility kills it).

### 11.9 Empty states
Every list view (`/members`, `/payments`, `/classes`, `/events`, `/store`,
`/messaging`) needs a real empty state:
- Icon (bi-* large, 48-64px, in `#94a3b8`).
- One-line description of what this section is.
- One primary CTA to add the first item.

Example for `/members`:
> 🎯 No members yet.
> Add your first one and start tracking attendance, payments, and ranks.
> [+ Add member]

## 12. Copy guidelines

### 12.1 Principles
1. **Active voice, present tense.** "Charge $99" beats "$99 will be charged".
2. **You-first.** Talk to the user, not about them.
3. **One idea per sentence.** Sentences over 18 words: split.
4. **Cut the polite filler.** "Please" once per flow, max.
5. **Show next step in error.** "Card declined. Try a different card or send a payment link."
6. **Confirm short.** "Saved." Not "Your changes have been saved successfully!"
7. **Numbers in numerals.** "3 members late" beats "Three members late".

### 12.2 Title-case rules
- **Sentence case** for almost everything (buttons, labels, body, navigation).
  - ✅ "Add member"
  - ✅ "Forgot your password?"
- **Title Case** for proper-noun product features and the brand:
  - ✅ "Marcos AI", "Member Portal", "Open Mat".
- **All caps** only for tiny eyebrows (max 11px), with letter-spacing.
- **Never** all caps for buttons or headings.

### 12.3 CTA vocabulary

| Action | Use | Don't use |
|---|---|---|
| Save | "Save" | "Submit", "Confirm" |
| Add | "Add member", "Add class" | "New +", "Create" |
| Pay | "Pay $X.XX" | "Confirm payment", "Process payment" |
| Charge | "Charge $X.XX" | "Run charge" |
| Delete | "Delete" → confirmation modal | "Remove", "Trash" |
| Cancel | "Cancel" | "Back", "Close" |
| Sign in | "Sign in" | "Log in" (consistency) |
| Sign up | "Sign up" or "Start free trial" | "Register" |
| Continue | "Continue" | "Next →" (unless wizard) |
| Send | "Send invite", "Send receipt" | "Dispatch", "Trigger" |

### 12.4 Error message recipe
**[What happened]. [What to do.]**
- ✅ "Card declined by your bank. Try another card, or send a payment link."
- ❌ "Error: card_declined"
- ❌ "Something went wrong."
- ❌ "An unexpected error has occurred. Please contact support."

### 12.5 Success message recipe
**[Confirmation]. [Optional what-next link.]**
- ✅ "Member added. [Add another →]"
- ✅ "Payment recorded. [View receipt]"
- ❌ "Success!"

### 12.6 Empty list recipe
**[What this is]. [Why you'd want one]. [Primary CTA.]**

### 12.7 Onboarding microcopy
| Screen | Headline | Sub | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Welcome | "Welcome to Fit4Academy." | "Let's get your gym up in 5 minutes." | "Get started" |
| Add gym info | "What do we call your gym?" | "We'll show this to your members." | "Continue" |
| Add first member | "Add your first member." | "You can import the rest from a CSV later." | "Add member" |
| Done | "You're live." | "Your gym is ready. Want a tour, or want to dive in?" | "Tour" / "Skip to dashboard" |

### 12.8 Email & SMS
**Subject line rules** (email):
- Max 50 characters.
- No emoji decoration. Semantic emoji is allowed (e.g. 📊 for a report).
- Specific over clever. "March receipt — $129.30" beats "Your monthly summary is here!"

**Body** (email):
- 1 line greeting → 1 paragraph context → 1 big CTA → 1 line sign-off.
- Sign-off: `— Fit4Academy` or `— [Coach name]` if it's owner-facing personal.
- No long legal footer. Unsubscribe link required for marketing emails (CAN-SPAM).

**SMS** (Twilio):
- Stay under 160 chars (single segment, costs less).
- Include: gym name, amount/action, link.
- Template (payment link):
  > "Hi {firstname}! {gym} sent a payment request for ${amount}. Pay securely: {link}"

## 14. Product brand

### 14.1 In-product brand surface
The product itself is the strongest brand asset. Every screen should feel
like it's part of one product family. Consistency rules:
- All buttons follow section 11.1.
- All input forms follow section 11.4.
- All cards follow section 11.5.
- All empty states follow section 11.9.
- Page header: F4 lockup left, gym logo upload right, single hairline divider below.

### 14.2 Marcos AI — the in-product co-pilot
**Persona**:
- Black belt who became a business consultant.
- Direct, confident, dry humor. "Retention dropped 8% — that's two stripes worth. Want to know why?"
- Always ends with a concrete next step.
- **Never pretends to be human.** When asked, says: "I'm Marcos, Fit4Academy's AI."

**Visual**:
- Avatar: stylized headshot or geometric mark, brand green accent.
- Chat bubble: brand green for Marcos's messages, slate for the user's.
- Always identified by name + small `AI` badge.

**Boundaries**:
- Never gives medical, legal, or tax advice. Routes to a human.
- Never makes claims about specific gyms ("Gym X is doing Y better").
- Always grounded in the gym's own data.

### 14.3 Member Portal
The student-facing surface. Branded as "your gym's portal", not as
Fit4Academy. Customizable per academy:
- Primary color (defaults to brand green if academy doesn't set one).
- Gym logo prominent.
- Fit4Academy attribution: small footer "Powered by Fit4Academy".

### 14.4 Payment surfaces
The most sensitive brand moment. Members judge the gym based on how the
payment screen looks.
- Payment link page: clean, dark, brand-green CTA.
- Receipts: gym name big, Fit4Academy small.
- Email receipts: HTML version with brand template, plain-text fallback.

### 14.5 Onboarding north star
**60 seconds** from sign-up to "I added my first member."
- Skip the tour.
- Skip the 12-step checklist.
- One clear next action at every step.

### 14.6 Trial expiration
Trial ends with a soft landing, not a hard wall:
- Day 23: email — "7 days left. Here's what you've done so far."
- Day 28: email — "2 days left. Add a payment method to keep going."
- Day 31+: app shows banner "Trial ended. Add billing to continue." but
  doesn't lock data. Read-only for 30 more days, then offer export.

## 15. Sales & customer success

### 15.1 Sales voice
Same as the brand voice (confident, direct), but slightly warmer in 1:1
conversations. Use the customer's first name. Use their gym's name. Mirror
their language (English / Portuguese / Spanish — don't switch unless they
do).

### 15.2 Demo script outline
1. **Discover** (5 min): "How do you handle billing today?"
2. **Show the mess** (2 min): empathize with their current setup.
3. **Show the cleanup** (12 min): live demo with their own gym name pre-loaded.
4. **Pricing in 30 seconds** (1 min): flat $0.30/transaction, no per-member.
5. **Trial start** (5 min): hand them the URL, walk them through their first member.

### 15.3 Sales objection bank
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already use Mindbody." | "How long did onboarding take? And what's your monthly bill including add-ons?" |
| "I'm fine with my spreadsheet." | "What does Tuesday morning look like when 5 members miss payment?" |
| "We're too small." | "We have gyms with 22 members. The trial is free." |
| "Will my members hate switching?" | "Members never see a switch. They see a portal with your logo." |
| "What if you go down?" | "Status page at status.fit4academy.com. SLA 99.9%. Stripe processes payments directly so even our app being down doesn't break your money." |
| "Spanish/Portuguese support?" | "Yes. Real support, real product, not Google Translate." |

### 15.4 Customer success
- First 7 days: white-glove. Onboarding call offered to anyone above 50 members.
- 30-day trial check-in: an email asking "what's working / what's not".
- Quarterly review: an in-app email with year-on-year stats and Marcos's three suggestions.

### 15.5 Support voice
- Empathy first sentence. Action second.
- Use the customer's first name.
- Sign off with the support agent's first name + "Fit4Academy team".
- Never "Dear customer" or "Hello user".

## 17. Legal, trademark, compliance

### 17.1 Trademark
- File **Fit4Academy** word mark in USPTO Class 042 (SaaS, software services) and Class 035 (business management, advertising).
- File F4 shield logo as a design mark in the same classes.
- Reserve common typos: `fit4academy.com`, `fitforacademy.com`, `fit4academies.com`.
- Defensive registrations in Brazil (INPI) and Mexico (IMPI) given LATAM-adjacent market.

### 17.2 Required pages
| Page | URL | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| Terms of Service | `/terms` | Use of platform, fees, liability, disputes |
| Privacy Policy | `/privacy` | Data collection, GDPR/CCPA, retention |
| Cookies | `/cookies` | Cookie banner + policy (CCPA, EU if needed) |
| Acceptable Use | `/aup` | What gyms can't do (no cash businesses, no fraud) |
| Subprocessors | `/subprocessors` | Stripe, Twilio, Anthropic, Cloudinary, Railway |
| DPA | `/dpa` | Data processing agreement, available on request |
| Status | `status.fit4academy.com` | Uptime, incidents |
| Brand kit | `/brand` | Public download of logos and rules |

### 17.3 Compliance areas
- **PCI-DSS** — card numbers must NEVER touch our servers. Use Stripe Elements only. Current `payment_link.html` violates this — flagged in product roadmap as critical.
- **PCI-DSS SAQ A** — confirm we qualify (full Stripe-hosted card collection).
- **GDPR** — even though US-first, expat owners may serve EU members. Comply.
- **CCPA / CPRA** — California Consumer Privacy. "Do not sell my data" link in footer.
- **CAN-SPAM** — unsubscribe in every marketing email.
- **TCPA** — SMS requires opt-in; collect at member onboarding.
- **COPPA** — kids under 13. Don't store kid data without parent consent flow.
- **HIPAA** — N/A unless we add medical fields. Don't add medical fields.
- **State waivers** — events with paid registration must have liability waiver per state law (we already support this).

### 17.4 Insurance to carry
- E&O / Professional liability ($1M+).
- Cyber liability ($1M+).
- General liability ($1M+).
